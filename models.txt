Cargo Models
These cargo models provide the shapes used for deliverable cargo, but they don't have all of the game logic needed to act as true cargo. The preferred way to spawn cargo on the map is via an objective, as described on page 354.
As of season 5, Saber has removed cargo models from the list of model assets in the Editor. You can hand edit the map XML file to put them on your map, but as of season 6, the Editor also suppresses these models from the packed file.

Saber has added _static versions of some of the cargo models. These behave like regular models and not at all like cargo, so they can safely be used as scenery. These are marked below with +_static.

The light_sky_spot_01 model is in the shape of a black cube, but you're expected to bury the model so that it can't be seen. The model comes with a very dim spotlight that hovers 25 meters above the cube and shines down on it. It's basically a nightlight for a small area of the map so that terrain and objects in it can be seen without other sources of illumination (e.g. when a truck's headlights aren't pointed at it). Although this light can be seen under the right conditions in the Editor, I haven't managed to see its effects within the game, so it's unclear whether this model is ever useful.

Rocks
Rocks tend to look good at many scales. The stronger constraint on rock scale is the accuracy of the collision mesh when the player is rock crawling. A lot of small, overly detailed rocks are bad for performance. On the other hand, scaling up a simplified collision mesh causes the truck's tires to visually float above or sink into the rock surface.
Thus, each rock model has a recommended range of scales that it works best at. Some of these values are derived from Saber's recommendations, and some are my own. I've checked them all, but with the caveat that I'm driving blind when checking the shape of collision meshes. You might find that some orientations work better for rock crawling than others.
Rocks marked "bottomless" don't have a bottom surface, so they cannot be rotated arbitrarily

Farplane Models
Farplane models can be used as a backdrop beyond the edges of your map.

You can scale or shift the position of a farplane model as you like, although it is not intended to exactly match the edges of your terrain. Instead, the idea is that your map's terrain is high near the edges so the the player is never looking more than slightly downwards when looking beyond the terrain edges, and the farplane model is then visible in the distance.
Many farplane models have a low side. These are appropriate when used with background water that extends to the horizon (page 221).
